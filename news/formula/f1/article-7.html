🔥 Sizzling Track Temps in Bahrain FP1
April 11, 2025

The 2025 Bahrain Grand Prix’s FP1 turned up the heat—literally—as track temperatures soared past 46°C, pushing drivers and tires to the limit. The blistering Sakhir asphalt, baked under the desert sun, made grip a guessing game, with several drivers reporting “sliding everywhere.” Teams leaned heavily on long runs to gauge tire wear, knowing the cooler night conditions for qualifying and the race will shift the balance. Lando Norris still conquered the heat for P1, but others, like Ferrari’s Lewis Hamilton, battled twitchy cars in the scorching conditions. The high temps also tested driver endurance, with rookies like Luke Browning feeling the strain. As teams analyze data for FP2, mastering tire management in this furnace will be key. Bahrain’s desert is unforgiving—only the coolest heads will prevail.
