💥 Williams’ Costly Pitlane Mishap in Bahrain FP1
April 11, 2025

Williams started the 2025 Bahrain Grand Prix on the wrong foot, copping a €7,500 fine after a pitlane blunder in FP1. Alex Albon’s unsafe release into Yuki Tsunoda’s path sparked chaos, prompting stewards to act swiftly. To make matters worse, a separate incident saw rookie Luke Browning nearly collide with a dawdling Albon on track, triggering an impeding investigation. Team principal James Vowles called it “a sloppy session,” admitting communication breakdowns cost them dearly. Despite the drama, Albon salvaged a strong P4, and Browning impressed in P13. With Sakhir’s scorching 46°C track temps already testing teams, Williams can’t afford more missteps. The fine’s a sting, but it’s a wake-up call for a squad aiming to climb the midfield. Will they clean up their act before FP2?
